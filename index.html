Code Of Conduct

Terror Realm SMP Rules

By joining this server, you agree to the Code of Conduct for TR minecraft server, and that you are present in the TR discord server. 
Being in the Terror Realm discord server is required to play in the smp.

1. Follow the Discord Server Rules
You are expected to follow the same rules outlined in the Terror Realm discord server in the SMP as well. Please keep the chats clean and avoid using harsh language or being overly aggressive to others.  This applies to the world as well (structures, signs, etc.)

2. Respect Staff Instruction
Mods, Admins, and Operators are the ones enforcing rules and making sure everyone has a good time on the SMP. You are expected to follow instructions and respect their choices when solving problems or giving punishments to players.

3. No Griefing or Stealing
You cannot destroy, modify, or steal from other player’s structures. You must ask before attempting to do anything to anyone's property.

4. No Hacking or Cheating
The SMP comes with QoL plugins installed. With that being said, only other client sided QoL mods are allowed. (waypoints, mini maps, etc.) Anything that allows you to hack, cheat, or glitch abuse on the SMP will result in a hefty punishment ranging all the way to an immediate ban depending on the severity. (x-ray, flight, etc.)

5. PVP Rules
PvP in claimed chunks (safe zones) are restricted, unless stated otherwise or given one-time permission. You also must have equal or similar armor and tools to keep fights fair. The player must have some kind of armor to be able to pvp.

6. Gravestones and Waypoints
Do not rob other players' Gravestones when they die. You also cannot spam or abuse public waypoints.

7. Custom Music Discs
Do not use inappropriate audio when making custom music discs.

8. Claiming Land
You can now more easily claim an area land as yours using newly added commands on the SMP. These commands go heavily in depth as to what strangers and trusted people can do in your area. These commands were added to further prevent instances of griefing and stealing. However, you are expected to play fairly and not restrict absolutely everything to everyone with superficial permissions such as interacting with doors. In addition to this, you cannot claim excessive amounts of land unless you actually own it and manage it.

9. Respecting others Land
There is plenty of space for everyone on the SMP. You are expected to respect the land that other people have claimed and have made buildings on. You cannot build too close or into other players' buildings unless they allow you to do so. The same thing applies to building on claimed land; you must ask whoever claimed it if you are allowed to build a structure there.

To get a better idea on these rules, go to https://mc.terrorrealm.com

Waypoints

To claim a chunk, you do /claim
To get in settings, you do /claim settings
Here are some ground rules for waypoints in general:

  • For permissions regarding waypoints, here are some things to check on your settings:

    • PvP must be set to Enabled
    • Use of Ender Pearls must be set to Allowed
    • Use Lecterns must be set to Allowed
    • Use Bells must be set to Allowed
    • Damage Monsters must be set to Allowed
    • Use Redstone is recommended to be set to Allowed
    • Place/Ride/Destroy of Vehicles is recommended to be set to Allowed (If you don’t want one of them to be disabled, then please disable both unless you have a reason to keep one on)
    • Use Workstations must be set to Allowed
    • Modify Signs is recommended to be set to Restricted
    • Pickup Items must be set to Allowed (No exceptions)
    • Use of Doors must be set to Allowed
    • Trade with Villagers is recommended to be set to Allowed
    • Trample Crops is recommended to be set to Restricted
    • Use of Chorus Fruit must be set to Allowed
    • Drop Items must be set to Allowed (No exceptions)
    • Use of Fence Gates should be set to Allowed
    • Use Beds must be set to Allowed

    For claimed chunks, here are some things that are required to state when anyone enters your chunk (e.g sign)

    • PvP (if given permission to do so) is disabled
    • Use of Ender Pearls (if given permission to do so) is disabled
    • Damage Monsters (if given permission to do so) is disabled
    • Place/Ride/Destroy of Vehicles is disabled
    • Use of any door type (if given permission to do so) is restricted
    • Chorus Fruit (if given permission to do so) is restricted

    If there isn’t any sign to show the players whats restricted in a chunk, they will assume its allowed. These should be the only signs you should mark on your chunk(s) in order for the player to understand what they are getting into.

If you want to change any permissions in your chunks that have must be set to (excluding ones with No exceptions), ask one of the online staff members in the Minecraft Server, or ask Grant (Ronaldo dude with the super awesome event host role!) to see if you can change these options.

If you are setting up a public waypoint, you should tell staff you are doing so, so they can give exceptions to your chunks if needed. If you want to claim more chunks than you are given, also ask the staff/operators for that. Don’t abuse this function, as this can result in you losing your /claim privileges.
